Trolleys act as highly mobile surfaces that travel continuously between sterile and contaminated clinical zones. Without strict zoning and decontamination protocols, they can easily become primary vectors for cross-contamination.

Collection trolleys, often referred to as "dirty" trolleys, are used exclusively to transport contaminated reusable medical devices from the surgery to the decontamination area. Due to their high-risk payload, these trolleys must be thoroughly decontaminated immediately after every single transit. Staff must use a medical-grade detergent or an approved disinfectant wipe to systematically clean all structural surfaces. Meticulous attention must be paid to the handle grips and the undersides of the shelves, as these are common areas for unobserved splashes and bioburden to accumulate.

Conversely, distribution or "clean" trolleys are dedicated strictly to moving clean, packaged items into the steriliser, or transporting fully sterilised items securely to the sterile storage facility. To maintain environmental hygiene, these surfaces must be comprehensively cleaned at the conclusion of each clinical shift, or immediately if they become visibly soiled. Furthermore, the wheels and casters on all trolleys must be routinely checked and cleared of entangled hair, suture material, and floor debris. Impeded or sticky wheels require greater operator force to push, which significantly elevates the risk of manual handling injuries in the workplace.
